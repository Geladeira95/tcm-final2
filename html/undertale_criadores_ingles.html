Undertale is an indie game that became a cultural phenomenon since its release in 2015. Created by Toby Fox, an autodidact developer and composer, the game gained attention for its unique approach to the RPG genre, subverting many of the traditional conventions of video games. Toby Fox, who was initially an independent developer and composer for video game music, used his passion for storytelling and game design to create an experience that stands out not only for its gameplay but also for its emotional impact and originality.

Additionally, Undertale's soundtrack, composed by Toby Fox, is one of the game's greatest highlights. With music ranging from cheerful to eerie, the soundtrack not only complements the game's atmosphere but also became iconic in its own right, receiving praise from both critics and fans.

The story of Undertale takes place in an underground world, where the player takes on the role of a human who falls into a realm inhabited by monsters. The game allows the player to choose between a peaceful approach, attempting to talk and befriend the monsters, or taking a more aggressive path by defeating them in battles. The combat mechanics and narrative offer a deep reflection on morality, choices, and consequences, creating multiple endings based on the player's actions.

The success of Undertale can be attributed to Toby Fox's unique vision, who, with a limited budget and few resources, managed to create an experience that resonated deeply with players. His passion for storytelling and innovating within limitations is what made Undertale one of the greatest successes in the indie scene, establishing Toby Fox as one of the most influential creators in the contemporary gaming industry.
